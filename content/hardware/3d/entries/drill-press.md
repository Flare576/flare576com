## Well, Extensive Is A Bit Of A Stretch...

It basically linked me to a bunch of DIY spindle sanders on Youtube and around the internet, but it was through this conversation I realized that the _start_ of a spindle sander is really just a drill press, then you build a mount on the drill base for the axle to go through.

So, I wondered "Is there a way I can 3D print a drill press mount for my rotary tool?

### Yes. Yes you can.

[Here's what I built off of](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:1642711), and [here's what I added to the Internet](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:7216820).

Basically, the original post had no instructions, a vague and incorrect Bill of Materials (he listed M5 screws and all the parts use M6 screws, for example) and the clamps the model used wouldn't fit on my tool, so I fixed all those things and posted up a remix.

![It works!](https://youtu.be/mv6mbfXP1nQ)

The model allows you to rotate the tool mount 45 and 90 degrees, but really, really sloppily. I think if I actually use this more than a couple of times, I'm going to need to revisit the mounting mechanism to increase its rigidity before I try to "spindle sander" the thing, too, but at least it works!

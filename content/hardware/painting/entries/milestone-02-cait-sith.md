Remove them with a heat gun before priming, and use a piece of high-grit sand paper to remove the stubble before and after (you'll miss some).

## Errata

### Flow Medium

"Flow Medium" is not the same as [airbrush thinner](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0DHVG6FZ7?_encoding=UTF8&psc=1). Get the right stuff for the job.

```flare
"Flow Medium" won't wreck your airbrush or anything, but it's not made to do the same thing as Airbrush thinner - it solves different problems:

- If your paint dries too fast, add Flow Medium
- If you want transparent layers, add Flow Medium
- If you want to thin your paint without messing with the opacity or colors, use Airbrush thinner
```

### Cleaning Your Airbrush

First, take your airbrush apart before you use it the first time. You'll end up taking it apart eventually, and doing it BEFORE you've clogged it with paint will help you understand the pieces.

```flare
My airbrush's nozzles actually have THREE pieces. I would have known this if I'd have taken it apart before clogging it so badly that it seemed like two pieces.
```

Second - save the Acetone for last. It actually causes the paint to congeal, which can be great if that's your goal, but if you're trying to clean-while-spraying, it's counterproductive.

Third - leverage the fact that acrylic paint is water-based - stick the whole damn airbrush into warm water. Cleaning is WAY easier than I thought it was when I was doing it with a dropper and towel.

```flare
Obviously, make sure you don't lose parts of it down the drain.
```

### Your Spray Pot

You never need to fully tighten the lid while you're painting - leave it partially unscrewed so that, if you need to dump out your cup, you can take the lid off with one hand.

# Airbrush Saga Pt. 1

# Way Back When

Years and years ago, when I was just a lad of, oh, say ten, my dad was interested in Remote Controlled (RC) Cars.

Well, "Interested" in the way that I'm "interested" in video games and Steam Deck stuff - which is to say obsessed with and dabbling in anything even tangentially related to the subject.

One of the things he was dabbling in was painting RC car bodies, and he had a friend with a hobby shop in town that was willing to put his paint jobs up for sale at his shop.

He started with just an assortment of brushes, but eventually sold enough to afford a Model Master airbrush.

I'd link you to its product page, but it basically doesn't exist online.

![Model Master](/images/thumbnail/airbrush_master.jpg)

I think he had my brother and I use it to "Help" him paint once or twice, and somehow that memory stuck with me. While getting wrapped up in 3D printing and looking into post-processing options, I remembered that he used to have it, and asked if he still did.

# Wait, How Long Ago Did He Get That?

A while ago, young one. A. While. Ago.

He, like his sons, keeps 💩 well past the time normal people do, and he had this airbrush in a box for probably 25 years.

He was able to dig through nearly 3 decades worth of memories (and boxes) to find it, nestled into a box of similarly aged rattle cans, thinner, and other paint paraphernalia.

The airbrush totally still works, but the paint... Not so much.

One key component of airbrushing is right there in the name - "air." Though he kept his brush, he'd gotten rid of his compressor - which is probably for the best because by now it'd likely just be an explosion waiting to happen (I don't think they were using stainless steel or even Oil-free motors at the time).

This meant I'd have to bring my own to the party, and until now, that compressor had been a strictly "Outside" tool due to the noise.

I mean, it's labeled as "[Ultra Quiet](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00TDNKBMC)," but it's still a 1HP motor running indoors, so it isn't "Silent."

This meant that, if I wanted to paint without my family plotting my murder, I'd need to find an occasional alternative.

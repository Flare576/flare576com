# Back Story

Almost 1 year ago, Wizards of the Coast and SquareEnix released something that I was **very** excited for.

In fact, at the time, it was hard to remember a product that I had been _more_ excited for.

They released [Magic: The Gathering Final Fantasy](https://magic.wizards.com/en/products/final-fantasy).

I haven't talked a lot about MtG much in my writing, but it'd be difficult to tell you which consumed more of my time growing up between it and Final Fantasy...

...but it wouldn't be hard to tell you which consumed more of my money.

So, the fact that they were releasing a set of cards based on the games was, essentially, the worst possible thing to happen to my wallet.

## And Then Scalpers Ruined It

The cards sold out everywhere almost immediately, then were re-sold for anywhere from 3x to 5x, so a $60 Pre-constructed deck was over $200.

So, my excitement turned to frustration and anger, and I tried to just ignore how cool the cards ended up being... Which was hard 'cause

![Chocobo](https://media.wizards.com/2025/images/magic/fin/vnvWEig7q5IQXqxoN/CMmqI6Bt_055_baselayer.png)

They were pretty cool.

# Christmas 2025

Then my son made Christmas a little more awesome and got me [Counter Blitz](https://moxfield.com/decks/lrrdWuKxM0KM9uE9-x4LJw), a Commander deck all about moving +1/+1 counters around your board.

Which, ironically, was something he [already knew I enjoyed](https://moxfield.com/decks/qgrHhQlEkk-YnpE4bWfGpA) (that's a deck he built for me that **also** plays with +1/+1 counters!)

The only problem is that the deck is unreasonably good. I think it's win/loss rate is probably 10:1 so far.

# That's Good, Right?

Not really - it probably doesn't surprise you, but I don't really **enjoy** winning.

I enjoy a [Beautiful Game](https://www.reddit.com/r/EDH/comments/5i8jz4/what_does_playing_a_beautiful_game_mean_to_you/).

```flare
A close game, or a game where the outcome is surprising because of an unseen strategy. A game where an NFL team destroys a squad of toddlers is a great example of the opposite. The name of the concept comes from [The Wise Man's Fear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wise_Man%27s_Fear) by [Patrick Rothfuss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Rothfuss).
```

# So, What Now?

Well, one of the reasons that he was able to get me the deck is that the prices finally started to become more reasonable.

Not MSRP, but ...accessible.

So, I'm setting a goal for myself - I want to make enough selling stuff I **make** on eBay to afford a Booster Box of MtG:FF cards.

```flare
My collection of random Vines shit, games, books, guides, and Nintendo Power issues don't count.
```

# What Else Is There?

I've wanted to sell [Dummy 13](https://www.lucky13toys.com/13-series#h.4u06i7rod4p) models for a while now, so I'm going to actually [start doing that](https://www.ebay.com/itm/267692963199)!

I've also had this illusion of selling [Sofle Keyboards](https://github.com/Flare576/sofle) for a while now, so I'm using this as motivation to do that, too.

## Do People Actually Want Those Things?

No idea, but the beauty of both of those things is that **I REALLY LIKE THEM**, so I already have most of the stuff I need to make them - my up-front investment is mostly just time.
